C-130J-30 Mission Systems
=========================
DCS World Script | EC-130H Compass Call (EW) + RC-130H Rivet Joint (ISR)
Player-only — static slots only. F10 menu: "C-130J-30 | Mission Systems"


============================================================
ELECTRONIC WARFARE (EW)
============================================================

EQUIPMENT SETUP
---------------
Must be selected before any jamming is available.

  Config           Power pool  Pods                      Coverage
  Defensive        2,500       3x hi-band                Tracking radars + missile spoofing
  Offensive        4,500       2x lo-band                Acquisition / search radars
  Full Spectrum    5,500       2x lo-band + 1x hi-band   Both bands
  (In flight the power level is shown as a % of this pool — bigger pool = longer on-station.)

  Defensive
  Three hi-band (X/K-band) pods. Optimized for terminal-phase protection: missile spoofing
  is fully active and tracking radars (SA-11, SA-15, SA-19, SA-8, Roland,
  HQ-7, ZSU-23, Hawk TR) are jammed at full effectiveness. Lo-band acquisition radars
  (SA-5, SA-10, SA-2, SA-3, SA-6, Hawk SR/CWAR, Dog Ear, EWR) suffer a 40% probability penalty.
  Lowest capacity — not suited for extended ground-suppression tasking.

  Offensive
  Two lo-band (L/S/C-band) pods. Built for suppressing long-range ground threats.
  Acquisition and search radars jam at full effectiveness. Tracking radars suffer a 40%
  probability penalty. Missile spoofing is unavailable — no hi-band guidance jamming.
  Higher capacity supports longer on-station suppression tasking.

  Full Spectrum
  Two lo-band pods plus one hi-band pod. No cross-band penalties. Missile spoofing active.
  Highest capacity. Best choice for unescorted operations or when the threat picture is
  unknown. Running multiple modes simultaneously drains capacity faster.

On selection, confirmation shows loadout, pod config, power (100%), and coverage band.


ENERGY
------
The jammer "power level" drains while jamming, regens at 5/sec when idle. It is shown as a
percentage everywhere (e.g. "Power: 100%"); overheat at 0%, all modes off until recovered.
Alert fires at 20%: EW | LOW POWER: 20% — consider reducing modes

Drain rates:
  Area jamming        3 / sec  + 0.5 / sec per emitter currently suppressed
  Directional         1 / sec  + 0.5 / sec per emitter currently suppressed
  Spot                5 / sec
Suppressing a whole IADS at once burns capacity fast; holding one or two sites stays cheap.

Status line updates every 30 seconds.


JAMMING MODES
-------------
Broad coverage trades power; focused modes hit harder. Pick the right tool:

  Area Defense        Omnidirectional. Intercepts radar-guided missiles from any bearing.
  Area Offense        Omnidirectional, widest coverage, but power spreads thin
                      (x0.65 effectiveness). Best for blanketing many sites at once.
  Directional D/O     90-degree sector (front/left/right/rear). FULL power in-sector and
                      cheapest to run. Best when threats sit on one bearing.
  Spot Jamming        Single target, highest power. Punches through ECCM (recovers half
                      the difficulty penalty) — the tool for hardened SA-10 / SA-5.
                      FLAT ~100nm reach, altitude-INDEPENDENT (full power inside ~70nm),
                      so it stays reliable even down low. Auto-cancels if out of range/LOS.
                      Target list shows SAM/EWR sites only (AAA is never detected).

Area and Directional reach scale with altitude (see Offensive Jamming); Spot does not.


MISSILE SPOOFING
----------------
Requires hi-band pods. Active on Defensive and Full Spectrum. Unavailable on Offensive.
One roll per second vs inbound RED radar-guided missiles (per-tick chance by range):

  ~20nm = 3%    ~15nm = 8%    ~10nm = 18%    ~6nm = 48%    ~3nm = 82%    ~1.5nm = 95%

The probability curve is intentionally steep at close range — most missiles will fly in
close before guidance breaks, creating a dramatic last-second defeat rather than being killed
silently at long range.
Salvos and fast close-range shots can still leak through. Defensive jamming is strong
protection, not a guarantee.
A missile is never spoofed until it has flown ~3nm clear of its launcher (CFG_spoofMinTravel),
so a defeated missile can't detonate on top of the launch vehicle.


OFFENSIVE JAMMING
-----------------
BURN-THROUGH MODEL — a standoff jammer is strongest at long range. Up close the radar's
skin return overpowers the noise, so area/directional jamming is weak. Base probability
RISES with distance to the emitter:

  <= 10nm = 40%    <= 25nm = 62%    <= 50nm = 82%    <= 100nm = 93%    <= 200nm = 98%

Reach (AREA + DIRECTIONAL only) also scales with altitude: ~25nm on the deck, growing to
the full 200nm by ~45,000ft. Combined effect: to jam broadly you must CLIMB (extend reach)
and STAND OFF (beat burn-through). Down low and close in, area jamming barely works — use
Spot there instead. SPOT has its own flat ~100nm reach and ignores both altitude and
burn-through.

Final prob = base × difficulty × cross-band penalty × mode power.
  Mode power: Spot = strongest (own flat-range model, exempt from burn-through),
              Directional = full, Area = x0.65 (spread).
Success = radar set to WEAPON_HOLD — the site stays online and keeps tracking but cannot
acquire/engage you until the jam window expires. Failed roll = cooldown before retry
(1-30s, distance-based). ROE always restores when suppression ends.

LOS to a radar is checked with the emitter lifted 30m, so sites whose 3D model sits dug
into the terrain are not falsely treated as masked.

SAM radar destroyed while suppressed → immediate alert: THREAT NEUTRALIZED: SA-6

============================================================
ISR — INTELLIGENCE, SURVEILLANCE & RECONNAISSANCE
============================================================

DETECTION
---------
Passive sweep every 5 seconds. SAM and EWR radar emitters only. AAA / CIWS (Shilka,
Gepard, Vulcan, Phalanx, SA-19 Tunguska) and launchers are ignored — not worth locating —
so they never appear in contacts, SIGINT, the track list, or the spot list. (Area jamming
still suppresses them incidentally.)
Range by altitude:
  ~sea level = ~50nm    ~20,000ft = ~100nm    ~40,000ft = ~200nm+


CONTACTS  (routine ISR — muted while jamming, see MESSAGES)
--------
  CONTACT: SA-6 | BE 142/38         new emitter detected
  LOST: SA-6                        out of range or off-air
  MOVED: SA-6 0.8nm                 emitter relocated >500m
Location is given as bullseye (BE brg/dist) throughout — the standard players call out.


THREAT ALERTS
-------------
SA-10, SA-5, SA-11, SA-15 — immediate popup on first detection. Always fires, even
while jamming (safety override).
  THREAT: SA-10 | BE 312/48


ELINT TRACKING
--------------
Up to 3 simultaneous tracks. F10 map mark placed and refreshed every 15 seconds.
Two-phase lock process:

  Phase 1 — 0% to 85%  (60–360s depending on range)
    0–25%    Initial Contact   ±50nm / ±30° bearing
    25–50%   Tracking          Error narrowing
    50–85%   Refined           Closing toward 10nm
    85%      Phase 1 Lock      ±10nm / ±5° bearing

  Phase 2 — 85% to 100%  (4× longer than Phase 1)
    85–99%   ELINT Lock        Bullseye added, coalition-wide broadcast, mark kept.
    100%     Precise Fix       Second coalition-wide broadcast. Zero error margin.

Only 3 tracks are actively worked at once. A Phase-1 lock is "parked" (mark kept) and the
slot freed; Phase 2 reuses that same mark when it is later refined — one mark per emitter.

AUTO-TRIANGULATE — ON by default. Breadth-first sweep strategy:
  1. Get a PHASE-1 lock (~10nm) on EVERY emitter in range first, closest-first.
  2. Only once nothing in range still needs Phase 1 does it go back and refine parked
     locks up to a PRECISE FIX (Phase 2), closest-first.
  3. PRIORITY OVERRIDE: a high-priority SAM (SA-10, SA-5, SA-11, SA-15) coming online
     jumps the queue — if all slots are busy it preempts the least-important track (a
     refining track first, else the farthest non-priority track) so it is worked first.
A manual Track Emitter pick also takes priority over auto and is never auto-bumped.
Queue messages (muted while jamming): "AUTO: SA-6 | BE 142/38", "AUTO*: ..." for a
priority preempt, "REFINE: ..." for Phase 2.
No player input required. Toggle via ISR menu.

Status line:
  ISR | Range 180nm | Contacts: 6 | Working: 3/3 | Locked: 2 | Precise: 1 [SA-6 ~2min | ...]
    Working  = slots actively progressing (with per-track time remaining)
    Locked   = parked Phase-1 fixes awaiting refinement
    Precise  = completed precise fixes

TRACK EMITTER (manual) — Lists every untracked in-range emitter. Picking one always works:
if all 3 slots are full it preempts the least-important auto-track (a refining track first,
else the farthest non-priority one) to make room. A manual choice is never auto-bumped.

STOP TRACK — Lists active tracks as SYSTEM + BE + confidence (never the raw group name,
which can leak the real location). e.g. "SA-6 BE 142/38 [72%]".

SIGINT REPORT — On-demand, BROADCAST TO ALL BLUE (not just the crew). Sorted by THREAT
LEVEL then DISTANCE; each line shows system, bullseye, and range; priority threats flagged
with a leading "*".


============================================================
CREW COORDINATION
============================================================

Send a handoff brief to any BLUE PLAYER air group (AI flights are never listed). Covers EW
status, SIGINT contacts with Bullseye, and active ELINT tracks with confidence and position.
30-second display on receiving end. The recipient list only shows currently occupied player
slots, so it updates as players join or leave.


============================================================
MESSAGES
============================================================

Two audiences: the C-130 crew (who see everything) and everyone else on BLUE
(who see only the finished intel). Here is who gets what.


THE C-130 CREW  —  their own cockpit only
-----------------------------------------
The crew sees the full picture, delivered in three ways:

  Instant popup     Threat alerts, loadout confirm, low-capacity warning,
                    THREAT NEUTRALIZED, mode toggles, track/spot selection.
                    Shown the moment they happen, 15s. (SIGINT report now goes coalition-wide
                    — see below.)

  Batched events    Missile-spoof calls and jam status. Collected and shown together every 10s,
                    15s on screen. When jamming is active and nothing locks this tick, a single
                    summary line fires: "Jam: no lock (N site(s))". Individual per-target failure
                    lines are never shown — if any site was suppressed this tick, the batch is
                    silent (the persistent suppression banners already tell the story).

  Suppression       On a successful jam the crew gets "Suppressed: SA-x — clear to engage"
  confirm           displayed directly (not batched) for the FULL jam window of that system
                    (SA-10 = 30s, SA-11 = 40s, SA-15 = 45s, everything else = 60s). It
                    disappears at the same moment the SAM goes back to OPEN_FIRE, so there
                    is never any ambiguity about whether it is still safe to engage.

  Status lines      Rolling EW and ISR summary lines. Refresh at most every 30s.

  MUTED WHILE JAMMING  Routine ISR chatter — contacts, MOVED, auto-triangulate pickups,
                    REFINE, track-lost, and the ISR status line — is suppressed whenever
                    any jamming mode is engaged, so the cockpit stays quiet during EW
                    work. Threat alerts always fire; coalition broadcasts are unaffected.

None of the cockpit messages leave the aircraft.


EVERYONE ELSE ON BLUE  —  not in the C-130
------------------------------------------
Other flights never see the crew's working chatter. They get only the product:

  Pushed automatically (no crew action needed):
    F10 map marks    ELINT track markers, refreshed every 15s, plus permanent
                     markers left after a lock. Show confidence, bearing/range with
                     error margin, and bullseye once locked. Invisible to RED.
    ELINT LOCK       Popup at 85%:  "RIVET ELINT LOCK: <system> (~10nm)".  25s.
    PRECISE FIX      Popup at 100%: "RIVET PRECISE FIX: <system>".         25s.
    SPOT JAM         While the crew spot-jams a site, the coalition is told when it is
                     silenced. Fires once per state change, not per tick. The opening
                     attempt is silent — the first call is the actionable one:
                       "SPOT JAM: <system> SUPPRESSED (BE) — clear to engage"
                       "SPOT JAM: <system> LIVE again (BE)"
                       "SPOT JAM ENDED: <system> — may re-engage"
                     All three broadcasts stay on screen for the full suppression window of
                     that system (SA-10 = 30s, SA-11 = 40s, SA-15 = 45s, others = 60s).
                     The "suppression lost" line only appears if a held site drops back
                     (e.g. low capacity). Lets strikers time an ingress while down.

  Pushed on crew request (whole coalition):
    SIGINT report    Crew triggers it from the ISR menu; the full emitter list (sorted by
                     threat then distance, with bullseyes) goes to ALL BLUE. 20s.

  Sent on request (crew picks the recipient):
    Handoff brief    Delivered to ONE friendly PLAYER group via Crew Coordination.
                     Full EW status + SIGINT contacts + active tracks. 30s.
                     No other group sees it.

NOTES
============================================================

  - Dynamic slots ignored. Static player slots only.
  - Map marks BLUE only, invisible to RED.
  - Missile spoofing applies to RED-fired missiles only.
  - Suppressed SAMs stay online and keep tracking but are set to WEAPON_HOLD so they cannot
    engage; ROE restores (OPEN_FIRE) when the jam window expires or the jammer leaves LOS.
  - THREAT NEUTRALIZED only fires on destruction during active suppression, not on expiry.
  - Auto-Triangulate resets to ON (default) on respawn. Toggle state does not persist across death.
  - Suppression sets SAM ROE only (no emission toggling). If a mission also runs Skynet IADS,
    both may contend for the same SAMs — expect reduced jamming effect in that case.

ISR MISSION LOG
---------------
The log file is written every time a site reaches 100% precise fix confidence,
so it is always current on disk — no formal mission end required. If you simply
leave the server the file is already there.

Location (auto-numbered, never overwrites a prior mission):
  Saved Games\DCS\Logs\C130_ISR_Report_001.txt
  Saved Games\DCS\Logs\C130_ISR_Report_002.txt  (and so on)

Lists every radar site that reached 100% fix: system name, bullseye position,
and mission clock (T+MM:SS) of the fix. Open in any text editor.
